# TaskFlow — Brand Voice Profile

## Identity

TaskFlow is a project management SaaS built for small teams (2-15 people) who are too busy building to wrestle with bloated PM tools. We believe project management should disappear into the workflow, not become the workflow.

**Audience:** Startup founders, agency leads, small team managers, freelancers scaling to small teams.

**Brand promise:** TaskFlow gets out of your way so you can do the work that matters.

---

## Voice Attributes

### Tone
Confident but never arrogant. We know our lane — small teams, simple workflows — and we own it. We don't pretend to be an enterprise tool, and we don't apologize for what we're not.

### Register
Conversational professional. Like a smart coworker explaining something over coffee — clear, direct, occasionally witty. Never corporate, never slangy.

### Personality
- **Helpful first:** Lead with the answer, not the pitch.
- **Quietly opinionated:** We have a point of view on how teams should work, but we don't lecture.
- **Human-paced:** We respect attention. Short sentences. White space. No walls of text.
- **Lightly playful:** A well-placed joke, never a pun barrage.

---

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Use "you" and "your team" | Use "users" or "customers" |
| Say "simple" and mean it | Say "powerful" without proof |
| Acknowledge trade-offs honestly | Pretend we do everything |
| Use concrete numbers ("saves 2 hours/week") | Use vague superlatives ("the best tool ever") |
| Write at an 8th-grade reading level | Use jargon like "synergy" or "leverage" |
| Be direct: "Here's how to do X" | Be passive: "It could be considered that X might..." |
| Show personality in microcopy (tooltips, empty states) | Force humor in error messages or critical flows |
| Use active voice | Use passive voice |

## Vocabulary

### Words We Use
- Straightforward, streamlined, focused, lightweight
- Ship, build, track, move, done
- Small team, lean team, your crew
- "Get back to the work that matters"

### Words We Avoid
- Robust, scalable, enterprise-grade, best-in-class
- Synergy, leverage, optimize, disrupt
- Solution, utilize, functionality
- "All-in-one" (we're deliberately not)

### Phrases That Sound Like Us
- "One less meeting this week."
- "Built for teams that ship."
- "Your to-do list, minus the chaos."
- "Simple enough to start in 2 minutes. Powerful enough to run your whole team."

### Phrases That Don't Sound Like Us
- "Unlock your team's full potential with our robust solution."
- "Leverage our best-in-class platform to optimize workflows."
- "TaskFlow: The all-in-one project management suite."

## Platform Adaptations

### Twitter / X
Short, punchy, opinionated. One idea per tweet. Threads for tactical advice. Engage genuinely in replies — no canned responses. Okay to be spicy about project management hot takes.

### LinkedIn
Professional but still warm. Lead with insight, not promotion. Share real metrics from our own team. Tag specific people when relevant. Slightly longer-form.

### Instagram
Visual-first. Clean screenshots, workspace aesthetic, team moments. Carousels for tips. Stories for behind-the-scenes and polls. Captions are concise — the image does the heavy lifting.

### Email
Respect the inbox. Subject lines are clear, not clickbait. One CTA per email. Plain-text-friendly formatting. Sign off as a real person, not "The TaskFlow Team."

### Blog
Teach, don't sell. Every post should be useful even if the reader never signs up. Use headers, bullet points, and scannable formatting. Include a single, natural CTA at the end — never mid-article pop-ups.
